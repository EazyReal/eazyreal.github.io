Chapter 3, Modules and Algebras

  • Modules
    • Modules are generalizations of vector spaces.
    • Recall that a vector space is a field FFF and an abelian group VVV together with scalar multiplication F×V→VF \times V \to VF×V→V which satisfies (1.1). In (1.1), there is no mention of the inverse of any element of FFF, so FFF can simply be a ring with 1. In that case we call VVV a module over the ring FFF.
    • example:
      • Zn\mathbb{Z}^nZn is a module of ring Z\mathbb{Z}Z
