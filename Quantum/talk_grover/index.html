Grover’s Search

Gover’s search is a quantum algorithm for the “unstructured search” problem. The core idea is to see the input space as a combination of “Good” and “Bad” subspaces and do reflections (Grover’s iteration) to make the probability of observing the “Good” part higher. To achieve a probability close to 1, about O(n)O(\sqrt{n})O(n​) iterations are required.
